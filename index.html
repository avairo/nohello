no hello

please don't say just hello in chat

Imagine calling someone mid-cook, dropping a “sup” and ghosting? That’s like tossing the methylamine and walking out — chaos, bitch!

❌ Don't do this

Walt 2:15 PM
hi
Jesse 2:19 PM
...?
Walt 2:20 PM
what time was taht thing again?
Jesse 2:20 PM
oh - 3:30 mate

Walt could’ve got his answer way sooner, and Jesse wouldn’t have been sitting there wondering what’s up. Instead, Walt made him wait just to ask the real question.

People think they’re being polite by easing in with “yo” or “sup,” like they’re in person — but this ain’t the lab, yo. Typing’s slow. So even if you mean well, you’re just making the other person wait for the real question, which wastes time (and yeah, kinda annoying).

The same goes for:

  • "yo, u there?"
  • "sup aswin – quick thing."
  • "You got a sec?"
  • "yt?"
  • "ping"
  • etc.

Just ask the question! 😫

✅ Instead try this

Jane 2:15 PM
Hiya! What time was that thing?
Jesse 2:15 PM
hey, 3:30
Jane 2:15 PM
Ta - seeya then!
Jesse 2:16 PM
👌 np

If you feel it's a bit brusque to simply say "Hi" and ask the question, you can still preface your message with as many pleasantries as you see fit.

For example:

  • "yo man, what’s up? also, when’s that deal goin’ down?"
  • "hey there! hope you’re good — you know what time we meet?"
  • "yo, if you’re not busy, can you check that cook schedule?"
  • etc.

Asking straight up saves time, yo. If the other guy’s outta the lab, they can still hit you back later — instead of staring at a lonely “yo” wondering what batch you’re talking about.

Do it right — everyone’s chill, yo. 🎉
